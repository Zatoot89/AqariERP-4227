# Aqari ERP Finance Lifecycle

## Scope

The finance layer converts approved real-estate transactions into receivables, invoices, receipts, expenses, commission obligations, and management reports. It is an operational finance subledger rather than a replacement for a statutory general ledger.

## Core invariants

1. Every row belongs to one agency and all linked contacts, properties, units, contracts, staff, and documents must belong to the same agency.
2. Posted receipts, allocations, issued invoices, paid expenses, approved commissions, and finance events are never physically deleted.
3. Receipt allocations cannot exceed either the unallocated receipt balance or the invoice balance.
4. Invoice balance is derived transactionally from posted allocations and cannot become negative.
5. A schedule item's paid amount cannot exceed its amount.
6. Commission split percentages cannot exceed 100%, and split amounts cannot exceed the gross commission.
7. Sensitive transitions—voiding money documents, approving expenses, paying expenses, approving commissions, and paying commissions—require a manager or administrator.
8. Currency is never silently converted. Reports group values by currency unless an explicit exchange-rate snapshot exists.

## State machines

### Payment schedule

```text
draft -> active -> completed
  |         -> cancelled
  -> cancelled
```

- Draft schedules and items may be edited.
- Activation freezes item amounts/dates.
- Completion occurs when every non-waived item is paid.

### Invoice

```text
draft -> issued -> partially_paid -> paid
  |         |             |          
  |         -> overdue    -> overdue
  -> void   -> void
```

- Draft invoices are editable.
- Issuing freezes line values and assigns issue metadata.
- Payment allocation changes balance and status atomically.
- Only managers/admins may void an issued invoice, and only when no active receipt allocation remains.

### Receipt

```text
posted -> void
```

- A receipt is posted when money is recorded.
- Allocations are append-only except through a manager-controlled reversal.
- Voiding reverses allocations and records a new audit/event trail; the original receipt remains visible.

### Expense

```text
draft -> submitted -> approved -> paid
  |           |           |      -> void
  |           -> rejected
  -> cancelled
```

- Agents may prepare/submit expenses.
- Managers/admins approve, reject, pay, or void.
- Property/unit/vendor links are optional but tenant-validated.

### Commission

```text
draft -> pending_approval -> approved -> partially_paid -> paid
  |                |            |            -> cancelled
  |                -> rejected
  -> cancelled
```

- Commission may be fixed or percentage-based.
- Splits can target internal profiles or external broker contacts.
- Approval freezes gross amount and splits.
- Payouts update split and commission balances atomically.

## Contract integration

- Lease schedules can be generated by monthly, quarterly, semiannual, annual, or custom installments.
- Sale schedules can be generated from sale milestones.
- Reservation deposits can become receipts allocated to the resulting contract invoice.
- Source references remain stable: `lease`, `sale`, `reservation`, or `manual` plus source ID.

## Numbering

Agency/year sequences use:

- Payment schedule: `SCH-YYYY-000001`
- Invoice: `INV-YYYY-000001`
- Receipt: `RCT-YYYY-000001`
- Expense: `EXP-YYYY-000001`
- Commission: `COM-YYYY-000001`

The sequence increment and record insert occur in one database transaction.

## Reconciliation

Daily reconciliation checks:

- invoice total = subtotal + tax - discount;
- invoice balance = total - active allocations;
- receipt allocated + receipt unallocated = receipt amount;
- schedule item paid amount = active allocations attributed to the item;
- commission paid amount = active payouts;
- no cross-currency allocation;
- no terminal document has mutable commercial fields.

The reconciliation API returns discrepancies without silently correcting historical data. Corrective actions create reversals or adjustment records.

## Reporting

Reports are currency-separated and include:

- receivables aging: current, 1–30, 31–60, 61–90, and 90+ days overdue;
- collected cash by period and payment method;
- approved/paid expenses by category and property;
- earned, approved, and outstanding commission;
- schedule collection progress;
- transaction profitability summary when both revenue and linked expenses exist.

## Export and statutory accounting

Invoices, receipts, schedules, expenses, commission statements, and reports support CSV export. A future accounting connector can map immutable finance events into a statutory double-entry ledger without changing operational records.
